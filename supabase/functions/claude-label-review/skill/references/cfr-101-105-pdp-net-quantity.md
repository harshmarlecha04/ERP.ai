# 21 CFR 101.105 — Principal Display Panel & Net Quantity of Contents

This is the reference for two related label requirements: (1) the **Net Quantity of Contents** declaration, and (2) the **PDP layout** — what must appear on the front panel and how much space non-mandatory elements can occupy. Load this for every label review; both apply to every dietary supplement package.

The most common failure is the net quantity declaration itself (often missing units, in wrong size, or in the wrong position). The PDP layout failure is usually a barcode or vignette taking up more space than allowed.

---

## 1. What must appear on the PDP

The Principal Display Panel is the part of the label most likely to be displayed at retail. For dietary supplements it must show:

1. **Statement of Identity** — the common or usual name of the product, plus "Dietary Supplement" (or the type, e.g., "Vitamin C Dietary Supplement"). Per 21 CFR 101.3, the statement of identity is the most prominent feature on the PDP.
2. **Net Quantity of Contents** — see §2 below.
3. **Brand name** — typically the most visible element, but not regulated.

The phrase "Dietary Supplement" can appear elsewhere if it's part of the statement of identity (e.g., "Calcium Dietary Supplement"). It doesn't need to be on the PDP if the type description (e.g., "Multivitamin") implies dietary supplement category and "Dietary Supplement" appears prominently on the info panel.

**Common failure:** "Dietary Supplement" missing entirely, or buried in 6-pt type. Flag as Major under §101.3.

## 2. Net Quantity of Contents — §101.105

### 2.1 What units to use

Per §101.105(b), the net quantity can be expressed by:
- **Weight** (avoirdupois pound and ounce)
- **Volume / fluid measure** (gallon, quart, pint, fluid ounce; or metric liter/mL)
- **Numerical count** alone — acceptable for solid items where count gives an accurate indication of contents
- **Combination of count + weight or measure** — required when count alone doesn't fairly indicate contents

For solid supplement gummies, capsules, and tablets, **count alone (e.g., "90 Gummies") is generally acceptable** because count is the meaningful unit for dosing. Adding weight (e.g., "90 Gummies (270 g)") is **good practice but not strictly required** by §101.105 if count gives an accurate indication.

**Note on Supplement Facts vs PDP:** the Supplement Facts panel must declare serving size in BOTH common household measure AND metric weight per §101.36(b)(2)(ii)(A) — e.g., "Serving Size: 3 Gummies (9 g)". This is separate from the PDP net quantity and is non-negotiable for the panel.

**Common failure (and common overreach):**
- *Real finding:* No net quantity statement at all on the PDP. Flag as **Critical** under §101.105(a).
- *Real finding:* Supplement Facts panel serving size missing the gram weight. Flag as **Major** under §101.36(b)(2)(ii)(A).
- *Overreach to avoid:* Insisting that "90 Gummies" alone on the PDP is non-compliant — it is acceptable per §101.105 when count fairly represents contents. Don't invent a violation here.

### 2.2 Metric units

Since the 1994 NLEA, both **US customary** and **metric** units are required where weight or volume is declared. Count-only declarations do not need a metric equivalent.

### 2.3 Type size — §101.105(i)

Minimum type size for the net quantity statement depends on the PDP area:

| PDP area (sq in) | Min type size |
|---|---|
| ≤ 5 | 1/16 inch (1.6 mm) |
| > 5 to ≤ 25 | 1/8 inch (3.2 mm) |
| > 25 to ≤ 100 | 3/16 inch (4.8 mm) |
| > 100 to ≤ 400 | 1/4 inch (6.4 mm) |
| > 400 | 1/2 inch (12.7 mm) |

The "1/16 inch" rule applies based on the **lowercase "o" height** (x-height), not capital letter height.

**Common failure:** Net quantity declared in tiny stylized type that fails the height requirement. Flag as Major under §101.105(i).

### 2.4 Placement — §101.105(f)

The net quantity declaration must appear in the **bottom 30%** of the PDP, parallel to the base of the package, separated from other printed matter by at least the height of the lettering used in the declaration (above and below) and by twice the width of the letter "N" (on the left and right).

**Common failure:** Net quantity buried in the middle of the PDP next to brand graphics. Flag as Major under §101.105(f).

## 3. PDP layout — non-mandatory elements

Per §101.105(f), non-mandatory label elements (UPC barcodes, vignettes, marketing graphics, certifications, etc.) must not be so prominent that they obscure or detract from the required label information.

The often-cited "**15% rule for barcodes**" is industry guidance, not an explicit regulation. The actual standard is that required elements (statement of identity, net quantity, "Dietary Supplement") must remain **prominent and conspicuous** per §101.15(a). If a barcode takes up 40% of the PDP and dwarfs the product name, that's a violation. If it's the size of a barcode normally is, it's fine.

**How to assess:**
- Look at the PDP image.
- Is the product name the most prominent text? It should be.
- Is "Dietary Supplement" visible without effort? It should be.
- Is the net quantity statement readable at arm's length? It should be.
- Does the barcode or any decoration push required elements to the margins? If yes, flag.

**Common failure (and common overreach):**
- *Real finding:* Brand name and graphics dominate; "Dietary Supplement" appears in 4-pt type at the bottom edge. Flag as Major under §101.15.
- *Overreach to avoid:* Flagging a normal-sized UPC barcode on a side or bottom of the PDP as "non-compliant" — the 15% number is heuristic; what matters is whether required elements remain prominent.

---

## 4. Severity calibration for §101.105 findings

- **Critical** — Missing net quantity declaration entirely; missing statement of identity; missing "Dietary Supplement" anywhere on the label.
- **Major** — Net quantity in wrong type size; net quantity not in the bottom 30%; "Dietary Supplement" present but not prominent.
- **Minor** — Stylistic preferences only (e.g., suggesting weight be added alongside count when count alone is sufficient).

---

## 5. Quick checklist

- [ ] Statement of Identity present on PDP and prominent
- [ ] "Dietary Supplement" present somewhere prominent (PDP or info panel, depending on statement of identity construction)
- [ ] Net quantity of contents declared on PDP
- [ ] Net quantity units appropriate (count alone OK for solid count-based items; weight or volume if applicable, with both US customary and metric)
- [ ] Net quantity type size meets the table in §2.3 above
- [ ] Net quantity placement in the bottom 30% of the PDP
- [ ] Non-mandatory elements (barcode, vignettes, certifications) don't dwarf required elements
- [ ] Don't invent a "net quantity must include grams" violation if count alone is acceptable
